Privacy Policy for DRAW

Effective Date: June 10, 2026

DRAW ("the App") is a two-player local multiplayer game for iOS. This Privacy Policy explains how the App handles information.

1. Information We Collect

DRAW does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information to us or any third party. The App does not require account creation, login, or any personally identifying information.

2. Camera Access

DRAW uses your device's camera during gameplay to detect your opponent on screen as part of the duel mechanic. Camera footage is processed entirely on your device in real time and is never recorded, stored, saved to your photo library, or transmitted anywhere.

3. Local Network Access

DRAW uses your device's local network (via Apple's MultipeerConnectivity framework) to connect directly to your opponent's device during a duel. This connection is peer-to-peer between the two devices on the same local network and is used only to synchronize gameplay, such as countdown timing and shot results. No data from this connection is sent to us or any third party — DRAW does not operate any servers.

4. Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, or transmit any data to third parties, because the App does not collect or transmit any data in the first place.

5. Children's Privacy

DRAW does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children under 13. Because the App collects no personal data, it complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar regulations.

6. Analytics and Advertising

DRAW does not include any analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected with an updated effective date at the top of this page.
